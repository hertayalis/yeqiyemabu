Adam Thielen Biography

Born on August 22, 1990, in Detroit Lakes, Minnesota, Thielen had a deep love for football and idolized the likes of Cris Carter and Randy Moss while growing up. He attended Detroit Lakes High School, where he participated in four sports, including football, basketball, baseball, and golf. He helped lead the Lakers to a 9-1 season in his senior year and was an All-conference and All-state selection.

After redshirting his freshman year at Minnesota State University, Mankato, Thielen played in nine games as a freshman in 2009, catching 21 passes for 252 yards and a touchdown. He continued to excel as a sophomore in 2010, starting in all 11 games and leading the team with 41 receptions for 686 yards and six touchdowns. In 2011, Thielen started all 12 games and hauled in 62 passes for 715 yards and five touchdowns, in addition to handling kick return and punt return duties.

During the 2012 season, Thielen became the main target for Minnesota State’s passing attack, and he led the team with 74 receptions for 1,176 yards and eight touchdowns. The Mavericks went on to finish with a 13-1 record, advancing to the NCAA Semi-finals. Thielen’s stellar performances earned him a spot in the NFL, and he signed with the Minnesota Vikings as an undrafted free agent in 2013.

In his rookie season, Thielen played in eight games, primarily on special teams, but was waived during the offseason. He re-signed with the Vikings, however, and worked his way up the depth chart, eventually earning a starting spot.

Over the next ten seasons, Thielen established himself as a reliable and consistent receiver, with 672 receptions for 8,925 yards and 79 touchdowns. He also holds several NFL records, including eight straight games of over 100+ yards receiving and 74 receptions in the first half of a season.

However, after the 2022 season, the Vikings released Thielen as a salary cap move, even though he was among the team’s all-time leaders in catches, receiving touchdowns, and receiving yardage. But Thielen’s talents were not overlooked for long, and he signed with the Carolina Panthers on March 19, 2023.
