Family And Education

In Tokyo, Japan, Murakami was born and reared. He loved anime and manga as a child and had always imagined working in the animation industry. He enrolled at Tokyo University of the Arts in order to acquire the drafting abilities necessary to operate as an animator.

However, he ultimately decided to focus on Nihonga, a ‘traditional’ form of Japanese painting that uses conventional Japanese artistic conventions, techniques, and subjects.

In 1988, he received his master’s degree. He then earned a PhD in Nihonga but gradually lost interest in its exclusive extremely political atmosphere and started looking into more contemporary artistic forms, media, and strategies.

Career

The situation of contemporary art in Japan dissatisfied Murakami, who saw it as “a deep exploitation of Western trends.” So much of his early work was satirical and socially critical in nature.

Performance art, parodies of the “message” art that was prominent in Japan in the early 1990s, and conceptual works are examples of works produced during this time.

In addition, he started creating his own pop symbol, “Mr. DOB,” which later evolved into a type of self-portraiture and was the first of several repeating themes that can be observed throughout his work.
