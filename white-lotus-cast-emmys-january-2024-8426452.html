'The White Lotus' Cast at the 75th Primetime Emmy Awards

June 26, 2024
blog

The sensational cast of HBO Max's The White Lotus showed up in showstopping style at the 75th Primetime Emmy Awards.

Jennifer Coolidge, Meghann Fahy, Sabrina Impacciatore, Aubrey Plaza, and Simona Tabasco — who all dominated the category for outstanding supporting actress in a drama series — as well as Beatrice Grannò, showed up in jaw-dropping looks that will surely be the fashion talk of the town.

Coolidge beat out her cast mates for the acting award wearing a custom Etro dress and thanking all the "Evil Gays" in her acceptance speech. Plaza set the internet a buzz in her buttery Loewe dress. Plus, the show's leading men leaned into more formal black tie attire, donning classic suits and tuxedos.

See ahead for all the stylish looks the cast of the White Lotus wore to the 75th Emmy Awards.

Jennifer Coolidge in Etro and Alexis Bittar earrings

Coolidge not only won the award for acting but also scored major style points for her flowy bohemian dress that was custom-made by Etro.

Theo James in Dolce & Gabbana

James smoldered in a fitted all-black Dolce & Gabbana look that was effortlessly chic. He wore a black resin and steel Omega watch to complete the look.

Aubrey Plaza in Loewe

Plaza polarized the internet in her stunning and structural Loewe dress that had many seeing blocks of butter and post-it notes.

Adam DiMarco in Dior Men

DiMarco was simply dashing in a suit by Dior Men. A silver logo pin and rings polished off the look.

Meghann Fahy in Armani Privé

Fahy flaunted her style in a sultry wine-hued Armani Privé dress with velvet roses entirely embroidered with crystals at the bustline.

Will Sharpe in Burberry

The social media-shy star stunned in a sharp suit from Burberry. Although he may have covered up his famous abs, he still gave us a hint of skin, forgoing the traditional dress shirt and tie.

Simona Tabasco in Marni

The Milanese fashion house outfitted the Italian actress in a floral collaged creation that simply wowed onlookers with whimsical charm.

Beatrice Grannò in Armani Privé

Grannò chose to wear a shimmering Giorgio Armani Privé black gown that overflowed at the neckline with romantic rosettes.

Leo Woodall in Giorgio Armani

Everyone's favorite nephew looked dashing in a  black double-breasted peak lapel tuxedo along with a white evening shirt from Giorgio Armani.

Sabrina Impacciatore in The Attico

The actress, who played the complex hotel manager, Valentina, wore a slinky long-sleeved dress from The Attico that featured bold cutouts and stunning sequins.
